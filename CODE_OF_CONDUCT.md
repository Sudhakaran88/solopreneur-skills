# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

This project welcomes contributors from every background, experience level, and walk of life. We want a space where it's easy to learn, easy to contribute, and easy to disagree without things getting personal.

## Expected Behaviour

- Be kind. Assume good intent.
- Be specific in feedback — critique work, not people.
- Be open to being wrong. Nobody ships perfect first drafts.
- Give credit where it's due. Link sources, cite research.
- Help newcomers. Everyone was new once.

## Unacceptable Behaviour

- Harassment, personal attacks, or discriminatory language.
- Trolling or deliberate disruption of discussions.
- Publishing others' private information without consent.
- Spam, self-promotion unrelated to the repo's purpose.
- Any other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This applies to all project spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions, and any community channel associated with the repo. It also applies when you're representing the project in public.

## Reporting

Report problems by:
- Opening an issue at https://github.com/Sudhakaran88/solopreneur-skills/issues (mark confidential if needed), or
- Contacting the maintainer directly on GitHub: [@Sudhakaran88](https://github.com/Sudhakaran88)

Reports will be reviewed promptly. All reports are kept confidential to the extent possible.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject contributions that violate this Code of Conduct, and may temporarily or permanently ban contributors for repeated or severe violations.

## Acknowledgements

This Code of Conduct is adapted from common open-source community standards, inspired by the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/).
